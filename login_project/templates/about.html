Our History

Laboratory management systems (LMS) have been around for several decades, but their evolution has been accelerated by advances in technology and the need for more efficient and effective laboratory operations. In the early days of LMS, they were primarily paper-based systems that managed sample tracking, test results, and equipment maintenance. As computers became more prevalent, these systems evolved into computerized versions that improved data management and analysis. Today's LMS are highly sophisticated software applications that can integrate with other laboratory systems, such as electronic laboratory notebooks (ELNs), laboratory information management systems (LIMS), and scientific data management systems (SDMS). They provide real-time monitoring and control of laboratory operations, workflow automation, and data analytics to help laboratories improve efficiency, reduce errors, and ensure regulatory compliance. With the increasing demand for accurate and timely laboratory testing and analysis, LMS are becoming essential tools for modern laboratory management.
